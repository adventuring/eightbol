Interworldly Adventuring Announces EIGHTBOL Compiler for Classic CPUs

A COBOL-shaped, object-oriented method language shipping assembly for 6502
family, Z80, Game Boy, Intellivision, 68000, x16, ARM Thumb, and more.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

ONLINE—1 April 2026—Interworldly Adventuring today publicly highlights
EIGHTBOL (Eight-Bit-Oriented Language), an open compiler that turns a strict
subset of COBOL into maintainable assembly for vintage and constrained
processors. EIGHTBOL is developed alongside Phantasia and the Skyline Tool
homebrew toolchain, and is published for community use at
https://github.com/adventuring/eightbol

EIGHTBOL targets, among others, the 6502 family (including 65c02, 65c816,
RP2A03, HuC6280), Z80, SM83, cp1610, Motorola 68000 (m68k), Intel 80286
(i286), ARM7 in Thumb-oriented form, and Fairchild F8—one front end, multiple
back ends, emitted as assembly sources for the developer’s existing build.

The language is built for object methods in a compact OOPS style: procedures
can INVOKE other objects, MOVE and COMPUTE state, branch with IF and EVALUATE,
iterate with PERFORM, and lean on required COPY inclusion for class layout
(copybooks). It complements narrative Fountain scripting where designers need
structured logic rather than screenplay flow alone.

Work on EIGHTBOL remains in progress: behavior and repository layout still
reflect Phantasia integration in places, and early adopters should expect
rough edges. Teams or individuals who would like to adopt EIGHTBOL for their
own ports or originals are invited to make contact via the channels above.

Is today’s date a wink, a milestone, or both? The compiler, tests, and
back ends are in the repository either way.

###

About EIGHTBOL
  EIGHTBOL is a MIT-licensed compiler (Common Lisp) that parses EIGHTBOL
  source, validates and optimizes an AST, and emits per-CPU assembly through
  dedicated back ends. It is a subset of COBOL—not a full standard
  implementation—with a feature set tuned to game object methods and classic
  hardware.

About Interworldly Adventuring
